Elliptic Orbits

With this calculator you can choose between the values you enter:

  • Perigee and Apogee Altitudes, meaning the Altitude of your ship in the highest and lowest points of the orbit
  • Perigee Altitude and Velocity, meaning how high and fast your ship is at the lowest point of the orbit
  • Apogee Altitude and Velocity, meaning how high and fast your ship is at the highest point of the orbit

The Calculator will return the following Values:

  • The Orbital Velocities of the current Orbit in the Perigee and the Apogee, meaning how fast your ship orbits around KEarth in the highest and lowest points of the orbit
  • The Orbital Altitudes of the current Orbit in the Perigee and the Apogee, meaning how high your ship orbits above KEarth in the highest and lowest points of the orbit
  • The Orbital Period of the current Orbit, meaning how much time your ship need to complete one orbit
  • The Orbital Eccentricity of the current Orbit, meaning how far the orbit is away from an ideal circular orbit.

If you press the 'Show Orbit' button, a window will open that shows a representation of the orbit around KEarth
